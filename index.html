The running example

"My terminal won't take payments"

A stressed merchant, customers in line, revenue stopped. A strong candidate for early validation: urgent, system-observable and potentially resolvable. It is the example that runs through every phase below, from hearing it on live calls to measuring whether the AI truly fixed it.

Understand how merchants actually reach for help

"You can't design the fix before you've heard the problem in the merchant's own words."

The call comes in

"It just stopped working!"
One sentence. Six different realities. Merchants describe symptoms; root causes live in systems.

What could it be?

Connectivity down
Wi-Fi, SIM or router
Card declines
The issuer, not the merchant
Device fault
Hardware or software
Account hold
Risk, KYC or settlement
Platform incident
Everyone's problem, not theirs
Wrong device
POS tablet or printer, not the terminal
But don't judge yet.
This is the core of discovery: stay wide before narrowing, and let evidence do the choosing.
Go wide before going deep
Collect every problem type without judging yet. Narrowing too early means solving the loudest problem, not the largest.
Facts over opinions
Every claim gets a source: a call heard, a ticket counted, an expert quoted. Assumptions are written down and marked for validation.

Four ways in, before any solution talk

Listen to live calls
How help is asked for in the moment, not described afterwards
Interview the experts
Support leads and product specialists, market by market
Mine the data
Tickets, transcripts and telemetry: volumes, patterns, peak times
Sit with the agents
Watch the workarounds; the unofficial fixes are the real spec
Where AI works in this phase illustration

AI clusters large transcript sets into themes overnight, quantifies volumes and surfaces outliers no human would find. Humans hear the frustration, the workarounds, and what merchants mean but don't say. Analysis is automated; judgment is not.

Phase 02 · Define Pick the right one, and the right tool Evidence picks the problem; the job picks the tool. Open this phaseClose this phase

Pick the problem worth solving, and check AI is the right tool

"Solving the wrong problem well is still failure."

Prioritize on evidence, not enthusiasm

Why "terminal down" first
A strong candidate for early validation because the problem is urgent, system-observable and potentially resolvable. Actual volume and feasibility would be confirmed from call, ticket and platform data.
Success defined before building
A resolution is a processed payment in telemetry within 15 minutes of call end, a provisional window to be re-baselined on Flatpay's transaction data. Agreed with stakeholders up front, measurable without asking anyone.

The right tool for each part of the job

Predictable → rules
Ticket routing, status lookups, callbacks. Deterministic automation. No AI needed, no AI used.
Messy language at scale → AI
Understanding a stressed merchant, classifying symptoms, composing signals. This is what takes AI.
Trust and exceptions → humans
Account holds, anger, anything twice-failed. The human path is designed, not a fallback.

The commitment this phase produces

1
problem, framed
The problem statement
One problem statement, one success metric, one scope: help a verified merchant get from "it stopped working" to taking payments again, and prove it happened. Everything out of scope is written down and parked, not forgotten.
Where AI works in this phase illustration

AI sizes the prize: counts affected calls, estimates minutes lost per merchant, simulates conversations to pressure-test the framing. Humans decide what gets built and what "good" means. The success metric is a stakeholder decision, never a model output.

The call, designed end to end

"Never ask the merchant what a system already knows."

The path every call follows

One canonical control flow with explicit gates, early exits and branch-specific playbooks: predictable before it is clever.

STEP 0 Stabilize Calm the urgency, set expectations
STEP 1 Verify caller Second factor before account data
STEP 2 Check signals Telemetry, payments, account, incidents
STEP 3 Route To one of six diagnosis branches
STEP 4 Fix & prove A real payment, not a promise
STEP 5 Close or handoff Warm transfer, nothing repeated

Where the call forks

One symptom, six diagnosis branches: step 3 routes every call to its own playbook.

Phase 04 · Validate Prove it before anyone hears it Six test layers between design and a live merchant call. Open this phaseClose this phase

Prove it works before a merchant ever hears it

"Cheap to kill early, expensive to kill late."

Smallest real version first

One branch, one market, text first
Connectivity recovery in Denmark as a text simulation, before voice, before five more markets
Learn per iteration, not per launch
Each cycle answers one question: does routing hold? Does recovery verify? Do merchants trust it?

Six test layers between design and a live call

1 · Logic tests
Every branch, gate and edge case as an executable test
2 · Transcript replay
Historic calls through the classifier, scored against known causes
3 · Adversarial callers
Furious, rambling, silent, wrong-device: scripted and survived
4 · Voice reliability
Noise, accents, interruptions, latency: measured, not assumed
5 · Shadow mode
AI proposes on live calls, humans act. Agreement is the gate
6 · Staged pilot
Lowest-risk branches first; account holds never launch autonomous

The honesty gate

Defined kill criteria, agreed in advance
If replay agreement or shadow-mode agreement stays under the bar after two redesign cycles, the branch does not launch. Killing a weak workflow in testing costs days; killing it in production costs merchant trust.
Where AI works in this phase illustration

AI generates the hostile test set: synthetic difficult callers, accent and noise variants, thousands of replayed conversations scored automatically. Humans set the bar and make the launch call. The model is tested by machines and judged by people.

Phase 05 · Launch & scale Earn autonomy step by step One branch and one market at a time, six market overlays. Open this phaseClose this phase

Autonomy is earned, one branch and one market at a time

"Trust arrives on foot and leaves on horseback."

Three gears of autonomy

1 · Shadow
AI suggests, humans act. Zero merchant risk, maximum learning
2 · Assisted
AI acts, humans monitor live and can step in mid-call
3 · Autonomous
Per branch, per market, only where the metrics earned it

One workflow, six market overlays

DKFIFRDEITUK
Localize behavior, not logic. Six forked workflows would drift apart in a quarter; one canonical flow with overlays keeps every improvement global.
What the overlay carries
Language and speech models · product suite (terminal vs. full POS bundle) · fiscal and compliance rules · local self-serve links · escalation queues and hours. Market support leads review every script before go-live.

Handover is part of the build

Living documentation
Workflows, permissions and edge cases anyone can read and challenge
Trained support teams
Agents know what the AI does, and get full context on every handoff
Guardrails that outlive me
Disclosure, GDPR, PCI and permission tiers enforced in the system, not in memory

The hard rule at every gear

The merchant never repeats themselves
Every escalation is a warm transfer with the full story attached: who they are, what they said, what was checked, what was tried, what was promised. A handoff that loses context is a failure, whatever the dashboard says.
Where AI works in this phase illustration

AI drafts market variants of scripts and self-serve content for expert review, and monitors rollout metrics in real time. Humans own go-live: each market's support lead signs off before their merchants hear a single AI call.

Phase 06 · Improve Launch is the beginning Measure honestly, fix weekly, let the metrics write the roadmap. Open this phaseClose this phase

Launch is the beginning: measure honestly, fix weekly

"Containment is not resolution."

The metric that cannot lie

15 min
A resolution is a processed payment
Seen in telemetry within 15 minutes of call end, for hardware and connectivity fixes; the window is a provisional target to be re-baselined on Flatpay's transaction data. An AI that "handled the call" while the terminal is still down has failed twice: it failed the merchant and hid the failure from the team.

Four dials watched weekly

Verified resolution
Per branch: which parts earn more autonomy
Escalation reasons
Coded, ranked: they are the build list
7-day repeats
The detector of fake fixes that bounce back
Trust, AI vs. human
Satisfaction on AI-closed calls must hold the line

The weekly loop

Failures get a name
Missing signal, missing branch, bad script, or model error. Each class has a different fix, and naming it stops the team from blaming "the AI" as a whole.
Invest where the math says
Next branches and new data sources ranked by calls affected times resolution lift. The escalation log, not opinions, writes the roadmap.
Baseline before launch
Today's resolution rate per branch is measured before the AI takes a single call. Effect is a delta, not a feeling.
Stay close to the calls
Weekly listening to AI-handled calls continues after launch. Metrics locate the failure; the merchant's voice explains it.

Why this method, in one sentence

Discover widely, define sharply, design for the worst call, prove before launch, scale what earns it, and let honest metrics write the roadmap.
Built without inside knowledge of Flatpay's systems: every dependency on internals is a marked assumption with a named validation path, ready to be corrected in week one through the expert interviews this role runs on. The full workflow specification behind phase 3 exists and is ready to discuss.
Where AI works in this phase illustration

AI classifies every failed call and drafts the weekly review; humans decide what ships. The same discipline that started this journey ends it: AI for scale, judgment for decisions.
